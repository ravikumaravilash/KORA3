Ayurveda

Discover the world's oldest holistic healthcare system that has guided wellness for over 5,000 years. Learn its origins, philosophy, principles, and modern relevance through KORA's educational platform.

The Origin of Ayurveda

Ayurveda, meaning "The Science of Life," is one of the world's oldest systems of medicine. It originated in India over 5,000 years ago and was passed down through generations by sages and scholars.

Ancient Beginnings

Ayurveda traces its roots to the sacred Vedas, especially the Atharva Veda. It combines medicine, philosophy, nutrition, yoga, and lifestyle practices to maintain harmony between the body, mind, and spirit.

Purpose

The primary objective of Ayurveda is not merely to cure disease but to preserve health, prevent illness, and promote a long, balanced, and fulfilling life.

History of Ayurveda

A journey through more than five millennia of traditional medical knowledge.

3000 BCE

Vedic Origins

The earliest Ayurvedic concepts emerged within the Vedic civilization and were preserved through oral tradition.

1500 BCE

Atharva Veda

Healing practices, medicinal herbs, surgery, and preventive healthcare became documented in the Atharva Veda.

600 BCE

Charaka & Sushruta

The Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita became two of Ayurveda's most influential medical texts, covering internal medicine and surgery.

Today

Modern Integration

Ayurveda is now practiced worldwide and is increasingly supported by scientific research, digital healthcare, and AI-powered wellness platforms like KORA.

The Eight Branches of Ayurveda

Ayurveda is divided into eight major branches, collectively known as Ashtanga Ayurveda. Each branch focuses on a specialized area of healthcare and wellness.

Kaya Chikitsa

Internal medicine dealing with diagnosis, digestion, metabolism, and treatment of diseases affecting the whole body.

Kaumarabhritya

Specialized care for infants, children, pregnancy, maternal health and child development.

Shalya Tantra

The branch of Ayurvedic surgery dealing with surgical procedures, trauma care and wound management.

Shalakya Tantra

Treatment of diseases related to the eyes, ears, nose, throat, head and neck.

Agada Tantra

The science of toxicology dealing with poisons, environmental toxins and emergency treatment.

Bhuta Vidya

Focuses on mental health, emotional wellbeing and psychological disorders using holistic approaches.

Rasayana

Rejuvenation therapy aimed at improving longevity, immunity, vitality and healthy ageing.

Vajikarana

Deals with reproductive health, fertility, vitality and overall quality of life.

The Three Doshas

According to Ayurveda, every individual is governed by three biological energies known as Doshas. Maintaining their balance is essential for good health.

Vata

Represents movement within the body and controls breathing, circulation, the nervous system and flexibility.

Pitta

Represents transformation and governs digestion, metabolism, body temperature, intelligence and energy production.

Kapha

Represents stability and structure, providing strength, lubrication, immunity and emotional balance.

The Five Elements

Ayurveda teaches that the universe and the human body are composed of five fundamental elements known as the Panchamahabhutas.

Space (Akasha)

Represents openness, communication and the spaces within the body.

Air (Vayu)

Responsible for movement, breathing, circulation and nervous system activity.

Fire (Agni)

Controls digestion, metabolism, vision and body temperature.

Water (Jala)

Maintains hydration, nourishment, lubrication and flexibility throughout the body.

Earth (Prithvi)

Provides strength, stability, growth and structural support to the body.

Dinacharya (Daily Routine)

Dinacharya refers to the ideal daily routine recommended in Ayurveda to maintain physical health, mental clarity, and overall wellbeing.

Early Rising

Wake up before sunrise to synchronize the body's biological clock with nature and improve energy levels throughout the day.

Morning Cleansing

Maintain personal hygiene through oral care, tongue cleaning, bathing, and hydration before beginning daily activities.

Exercise & Yoga

Practice yoga, stretching, breathing exercises and moderate physical activity to improve flexibility, strength and circulation.

Balanced Nutrition

Consume fresh, seasonal and nutritious meals appropriate to your body constitution while avoiding overeating.

Meditation

Daily meditation promotes emotional stability, improves concentration and supports mental wellbeing.

Proper Sleep

Maintain consistent sleeping habits to allow the body and mind to recover naturally every day.

Ayurveda in Modern Healthcare

Today, Ayurveda continues to evolve by combining traditional knowledge with scientific research and digital technology.

Scientific Research

Universities and research institutions worldwide continue to study Ayurvedic herbs, therapies and preventive healthcare methods.

Preventive Healthcare

Ayurveda focuses on preventing diseases through healthy lifestyle practices instead of treating illnesses after they occur.

Digital Healthcare

Artificial Intelligence, wearable devices and healthcare platforms are helping make Ayurvedic knowledge more accessible than ever.
